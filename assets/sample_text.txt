Metaphor and metonymy are not just decorative devices in language but are fundamental cognitive processes that shape how we conceptualize the world. According to the Conceptual Metaphor Theory, we map structure from a concrete source domain (like journey) onto an abstract target domain (like life). This grounding in physical experience allows us to reason about complex philosophical and social concepts using familiar embodied categories. In an academic context, the term 'mapping' refers specifically to this cognitive alignment of structures between domains, which differs from its everyday use related to physical maps.

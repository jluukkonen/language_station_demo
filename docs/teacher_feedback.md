### Questionnaire Responses

#### 1. Where do multilingual students struggle most in your courses (e.g., terminology, instructions, discussions)?
This seems to have to do a lot with culture. We teach terminology but do the majority of students take any notice? Instructions tend to be easier to follow when in writing than when given verbally (probably because there is space to check). Lastly, discussions seem to be less of an issue if the participants will confident in talking, confident in their level of proficiency.

### Future Tool Considerations

#### If you could have one tool to support multilingual teaching, what would it do?
**Groupwork.** The best way to learn is to cooperate with your peers. If that means across their different languages, it is all the better. Each of them is bound to understand one part of what the task requires and together they will, in all likelihood, be able to find a solution.

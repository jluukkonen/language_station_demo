# The "Winning Demo" Flow
## Language Station: Cognitive Multilingual Toolkit

**Goal:** Prove in 3 minutes that our tool saves teachers hours of prep time while providing deep, pedagogically-sound multilingual support based on cognitive linguistics.

---

### Step 1: The Hook (The Problem)
*   **Visual:** Display a dense, intimidating paragraph from a real UEF syllabus, academic paper, or lecture slide.
*   **Narrative:** *"Imagine you are an international student with B1 English trying to decipher this text. Now imagine you are the lecturer trying to make this accessible to a diverse classroom without spending 5 extra hours manually translating and adapting materials."*

### Step 2: The Action (The Solution)
*   **Visual:** The presenter (acting as the teacher) uploads this PDF/text into our **Language Station** Streamlit app.
*   **Narrative:** *"With one click, the Language Station processes the text using our custom NLP pipeline—specifically tailored to identify high cognitive load and enable translanguaging."*

### Step 3: The Magic (The Output)
*Showcase the three core pillars of the generated teacher dashboard:*

#### Tab 1: The Cognitive Glossary
*   **Feature:** It doesn't just list definitions; it highlights terminology with high *semantic variation* and *polysemy*. 
*   **Example to Show:** The tool extracts a word like "framework." It explains its specific academic meaning in the current context versus its common everyday meaning, providing Finnish scaffolding (e.g., "viitekehys").

#### Tab 2: The Adapted Text (Bilingual Bridge)
*   **Feature:** A side-by-side or easily switchable reading view.
*   **Example to Show:** On the left is the original dense text. On the right is a CEFR-adapted (A2/B1) simplified version. Show inline Finnish (or other language) hints that pop up when hovering over complex clauses or metaphors.

#### Tab 3: Actionable Pedagogy (The Translanguaging Lesson Plan)
*   **Feature:** The AI provides instant, text-specific translanguaging activities.
*   **Example to Show:** *"Based on this text, here is a suggested 10-minute activity: Jigsaw reading. Have students read the original English paragraph independently, then discuss the meaning of the extracted key terms in Finnish (or their native language), before summarizing the core concept back to the class in English."*

### Step 4: The Impact (The Closer)
*   **Narrative:** *"In exactly 5 seconds, we just saved a teacher 2 hours of prep work. More importantly, we transformed a rigid, monolingual document into a dynamic, multilingual lesson plan that actively embraces translanguaging and lowers the cognitive barrier to entry for all students."*
